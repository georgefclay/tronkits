A popular thing to say right now is "AI Slop". And I have to say that a lot of AI writing is "slop". But, does it have to be?

I use AI to write some of my blog posts and tutorials. AI is actually very good at creating technical content; it tends to avoid the usual AI "tics". Things like "this is the thing that everyone ...", or adding filler words where they don't sound natural. I could write the posts, but to be honest, I think AI does a better job, and in much less time.

Here is an example of a well written blog post, written by AI https://tronkits.com/blog/ohms-law-in-5-minutes . I did do a bit of editing, and pointed out two AI tics, Claude then cleaned up two more. With a little extra work, AI can do a nice job.

This is what I did. I gave Claude 3 essays that I wrote in college and 2 blog posts that I recently wrote. I asked it to create a file that summarized my voice. Now when I ask Claude to write an article I tell it to use my voice file. When the article is done, I clear out the obvious AI tells. I also make sure I agree with the content. Last, I have Claude add a byline that the post was written by AI and edited by me. I haven't done that on some of my past posts, but I will do it going forward.

Why do I bother with changing the voice? I'm not trying to hide the fact that it was written by AI. I just want the post to have the same feel/vibe as my writing. I guess I like the sound of my own voice (what man doesn't, LOL). I really just want it to sound more natural; AI tends to move towards its "center", which rarely sounds like a real person. I'm forcing it from the center and towards a real person.

If you see a post that says "I, George Clay, wrote ...", you will know I wrote it. I want to be completely clear about who wrote what. I'm not ashamed to use AI; it is a tool. I use an impact driver to drive in screws; that doesn't make me less of a man than using a screwdriver. What really bothers me is when I read something that is obviously AI, and it is signed by a person, like they wrote it.

Let me know if you think I'm crazy. (OK I am, but that is beside the point)

*I, George Clay, wrote this post in whole. AI was used to clean up my grammar/spelling, and point out any gaps in continuity.*

P.S. This wasn't part of my original post, but I had to add it. After writing it, I asked Claude to look for spelling/grammar/continuity errors. It pointed out a few spelling and grammar errors, which I fixed. Then it wanted to replace "tends to avoid" with "it avoids most of them but a few always slip through". Which sounded to me like injecting an AI tic into what I thought was a perfectly understandable sentence. Don't always believe what AI tells you!
